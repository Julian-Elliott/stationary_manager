# THE BUTTON and the GOOGOL envelope: both jokes depend on intent, so both need placement + gesture guards

**Priority:** P2  
**Filed by:** UX/Feel Architect (mobile-first, serious)  
**Labels:** narrator, input, thumb-zone, finale, easter-eggs

---

Two intent-critical interactions share a design principle: the payoff only works if the player *meant it*.

**THE BUTTON.** If it rises anywhere in the bottom thumb arc, a fumbled tap mid-combo will: spend one of only three scripted chaos payoffs on a player who didn't choose it, possibly stamp the 'pressed within 4s' MBTI diagnosis by accident, and — worst — make the three legalistic warnings retroactively incoherent ('I never pressed anything!'). Spec: (a) place the Button in a top corner, in the dead thumb zone — deliberate reach = deliberate sin, which is the whole bit; (b) require press-and-hold ~600ms with a visible commitment fill and the narrator escalating *during the hold* ('You're still doing this. You are — you're still doing it.'); (c) the first accidental brush gets a free warning bark, never a payoff. The hold-with-live-narration is not a guard rail bolted on; it's the best delivery mechanism the joke could have.

**The GOOGOL envelope.** The finale asks the player to drag to *their own desk* — a drop target that has never been a drop target in the preceding 14 minutes. Every drag habit built so far points at the six workers. If the manager desk isn't unmistakably lit — beacon glow, ghost arrow, generous hitbox (≥1.5x normal desk), and every other desk's drop affordance disabled — some fraction of players will feed their job offer to Deborah, or stall, at the emotional climax. Nobody should be able to fail the ending; the drag exists to make the player *complicit* in leaving, not to test them. Also: timers should already be dead here — confirm no request can expire and eat a stapler during the recruitment letter.

Acceptance: Button unreachable by accidental combo-taps in a 2-minute frantic play test; envelope drag completed first-try by someone who has never seen the finale.
