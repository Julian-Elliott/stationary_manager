# Day 6 TPS finale: define the delivery quantum — 14,000 paperclips is either 14 drags (a climax), 56 drags (a grind), or 0 drags (an idle screen)

**Priority:** P1  
**Filed by:** Systems Architect (game design, serious)  
**Labels:** finale, readability, pacing, automation, narrator

---

## The math
Dollar cost of the entire finale is trivial by design: 14,000 clips = 1 pallet ($15) + 4 boxes ($8); 2,000 sheets = 4 reams ($12); 1 stamp sheet ($5). ~$40 total. Correct — the finale's difficulty is logistical, not financial. **But that means the interaction count IS the finale**, and it is currently unspecified.

1. **Set the quantum explicitly.** If a clip bin holds 1,000 and one drag delivers a full bin, the finale is ~14 clip drags + 4 paper drags + 1 stamp drag ≈ 19 deliberate actions ≈ 60-90 seconds against the progress table. That is a climax. If bins hold 250, it is 56+ drags of one-thumb repetition — a repetitive-strain epilogue. If runners and Gary automate burst+carry (both online by Day 6), it is **zero** player actions and the finale is a catalog receipt.
2. **Resolve the automation arc on purpose.** Gary (Day 3) deletes the tap-burst verb; runners delete the drag verb. By Day 6 the player has automated themselves out of both core verbs — which is thematically perfect ("humans hired to do what one Excel macro did" cuts both ways) *if staged*: let runners visibly do the TPS labor while the player conducts, and reserve the GOOGOL envelope as the one act nobody can do for you. If that is not the intent, diegetically sideline the runners for Day 6 (the auditor impounds non-essential personnel for the reconciliation). Either is fine; the accident of one is not.
3. **The 10,000-clip odometer monologue will collide with the finale.** Cumulative clips cross 10,000 mid-TPS wave, firing a priority-3 letterbox freeze in the middle of the climax. Retrigger the paperclip-maximizer monologue on *ordering/bursting the pallet* instead — it becomes the pre-finale beat and foreshadows the 14k demand, which is strictly better placement for the same script.
4. **Softlock check on the one (1) commemorative stamp:** confirm it is fulfilled by the standard $5 sheet-of-100 (99 surplus is a good joke — surface it in the progress table and let the narrator invoice it), and is not a phantom catalog item that cannot be ordered.

## Acceptance criteria
Finale is completable in 60-120s of continuous, legible player action; the progress table visibly ticks on every delivery; no priority-3 beat interrupts an active TPS wave.
