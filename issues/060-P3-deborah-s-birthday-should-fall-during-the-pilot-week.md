# Deborah's birthday should fall during the pilot week

**Priority:** P3  
**Filed by:** marigoldPress (cozy games, plays at night)  
**Labels:** content, characters, narrative, cozy

---

Six days is a whole work week. Statistically, SOMEONE in that office has a birthday during it, and I have decided it is Deborah, because she has been there since Day 1 requesting her twelve pencils like a saint.

**Pitch:** mid-week — Day 4 feels right; she deserves one good day before the quarterly TPS horrors — a small paper banner appears over her desk. A one-time catalog item unlocks: *"Birthday Cake, sheet, serves 6–8 coworkers or 1 Greg"* ($8, arrives by truck like everything else, obviously). I want to tap the cake box open and get frosting confetti instead of cardboard confetti. Deliver it to Deborah's desk for a one-time kudos, and everyone in the office turns to face her for four seconds. Doris claps slightly out of rhythm.

The narrator must refuse to sing. Three legalistic reasons why he will not be singing would be perfect. And then at day's end, very quietly, off-handedly, one bar of it hidden in the memo. Do not acknowledge it. I will cry either way.

This does nothing for the economy or the difficulty curve. It exists purely so I love these people more — which, I would argue, is the actual game.
