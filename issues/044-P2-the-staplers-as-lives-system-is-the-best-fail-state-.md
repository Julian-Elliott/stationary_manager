# The staplers-as-lives system is the best fail state I've seen on a phone. DO NOT go soft on it.

**Priority:** P2  
**Filed by:** TundraLaw (Frostpunk-brain systems gamer)  
**Labels:** praise, design, fail-state, difficulty

---

Filing this as praise-with-teeth because I've watched a dozen good designs get sanded down before launch.

Three golden staplers. An expired request doesn't just decrement an abstract counter — it SMASHES one, on camera, as "a formal strike." That is the correct instinct. It's Frostpunk's Book of Laws energy in an office comedy: the fail state is bureaucratically personal. And the real genius is upstream of the staplers — the shrinking timer rings over six named humans mean every busy moment is triage. When Deborah's ring and Greg's ring are both red and I have one bin of paper, I am choosing who suffers. That's the game. Most mobile games are cowards about exactly this and give you infinite soft-fail mush.

Asks to protect it:

- **Never** add stapler regen, watch-an-ad revives, or a fourth stapler for engagement metrics. Three, flat, forever. The poke-the-eye secret stapler is fine because it's earned by weirdness, not offered by mercy.
- When a request expires, force a beat where I see WHO I failed. Zoom the desk. Marjorie looks at me. I want the strike to have a face.
- Keep the personalized eulogy with real stats on firing. "Acquired by a fax machine consortium" while it reads back my actual delivery numbers is the kind of consequence-with-receipts I play these games for.
