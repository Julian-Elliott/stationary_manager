# Fear: this economy has no teeth — if I'm never broke, the catalog is a menu, not a decision

**Priority:** P1  
**Filed by:** TundraLaw (Frostpunk-brain systems gamer)  
**Labels:** economy, balance, difficulty, design

---

Run the numbers with me. Pencils $1/dozen, paper $3/ream of 500, clips $2 per THOUSAND, and every delivery erupts in a literal coin fountain. Day 1's entire demand is 12 pencils — one dollar. Unless income is tuned brutally tight, by Day 3 I'm sitting on a cash pile and every "choice" in the catalog collapses into "buy the big one, obviously."

The $15 pallet of 10,000 clips is a great joke, but it should also be a great GAMBLE — real capital committed against a clip-drought that might not come, versus getting caught short when the TPS reconciliation demands 14,000. Right now I can't tell if it's a decision or a punchline with a price tag.

Asks:

- **A daily budget that carries over**, tuned so at least once per run I genuinely cannot afford both things I need — the REPLY-ALL STORM's 8 reams AND Doris's stamps. Make me pick. That pick is worth more than any set-piece.
- **Runner wages that can actually hurt.** $30 + $12/day is only a joke about human macros if payday can bankrupt me. Let over-hiring in week one strangle week two. Payday narration over a negative balance writes itself.
- **Punish hoarding.** You already have an auditor-impound event — aim it at stockpiles. Sitting on 10,000 clips "unaccounted for" should be exactly the kind of thing an auditor confiscates.
- Related automation creep: Intern Gary auto-opening boxes from Day 3 AND runners auto-carrying means two of my three verbs can be delegated by mid-game. Delegation should buy me bandwidth for HARDER decisions, not idle time. If Days 4-6 don't escalate past the freed-up thumb, the game starts playing itself.
