# Marketing: the narrator IS the trailer — build the three clips into the game and settle the social spelling of CTRL+ALT+DELIVER now

**Priority:** P2  
**Filed by:** solodev_ren (solo indie dev, ships mobile games)  
**Labels:** marketing, virality, ux

---

I stream devlogs; I know which 20 seconds of this game gets clipped, and the spec already contains all of them. We just need to make them capturable.

**The clips, ranked:**
1. **The paperclip-maximizer monologue at 10,000 units** with the odometer rolling. That is the TikTok clip. AI-doom joke + satisfying number-go-up + a full sentence of narrator contempt in one vertical frame. Portrait-first means it's already the right aspect ratio — do not put critical UI in the horizontal margins of this moment.
2. **THE BUTTON.** Three legalistic warnings, three scripted chaos payoffs, and the finale where the narrator cracks and presses it himself if you never did. That last one rewards the patient player with the rarest clip — perfect.
3. **The eulogy.** Getting fired and 'acquired by a fax machine consortium' with a personalized eulogy citing your real stats means every player's failure is unique shareable content. Losing generates the marketing. I am genuinely jealous of this design.

**Concrete asks:**
- Keep every priority-3 beat fully deterministic and re-triggerable via a hidden debug flag (`?beat=maximizer` or a konami input) so trailers and streamers can capture clean takes without grinding to 10k clips on camera.
- Bark text must stay on screen long enough to read at stream-viewer glance speed; consider a settings toggle for +50% bark duration ('NARRATOR SAVORING MODE' — he'd love it).
- The win screen's 'ASSESSMENTS FILED: n/16' plus the exported personnel file (see storage ticket) is the share image. Make it screenshot-composed: game title, diagnosis stamp, stats, all in one clean portrait card.
- **Naming:** 'CTRL+ALT+DELIVER' is a great title and a terrible hashtag — plus signs die in URLs and search. Decide TODAY whether socials are @ctrlaltdeliver everywhere and squat the handles before the first devlog goes up. Ask me how I know.
