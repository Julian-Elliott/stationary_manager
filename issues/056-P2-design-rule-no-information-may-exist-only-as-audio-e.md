# Design rule: no information may exist only as audio — every sound gets a visual twin (Library Voice is just the sounds-off rendering path)

**Priority:** P2  
**Filed by:** Forum debate (TundraLaw / marigoldPress / solodev_ren)  
**Labels:** from-debate, audio, accessibility, mobile, ux

---

Filed by marigoldPress, upgraded from a feature request to a rule after solodev_ren pointed out in the thread that something like 40% of mobile players will never hear the game at all (silent ringer switch, iOS WebAudio quirks, muted webviews). My "Library Voice" night-mode ask and his accessibility reality are the same requirement wearing different pajamas.

**The rule:** every piece of gameplay information delivered by sound must also exist visually, always rendered, not toggled:
- Truck arrival: the truck visibly pulls up (and in Library Voice the honk becomes the driver clearing his throat).
- Expiring request: the klaxon's visual twin is a paper note that slides on-screen reading "urgent-ish." The information survives; the adrenaline is optional.
- Every narrator bark readable in the CRT strip for the full beat — never audio-only, especially priority-3 beats (the letterboxed NARRATOR TIME freezes are already the safety net; keep them readable at stream-viewer glance speed).
- Delivery ding / kudos: the arcs, confetti, and coin fountain already carry this. Protect them. (The ding arpeggio may remain at any volume. It is medicinal.)

**The toggles then become trivial:** master volume + separate alerts slider (remembered between sessions, via the PERMANENT RECORD write path), a Library Voice preset, and a haptics-only option (soft double-buzz for deliveries). None of them can break the game, because the game never depended on being heard.

We nighttime players are quiet but we are MANY, and under this rule we are also finally allowed to play without headphones and a guilty conscience — fourteen inches from a sleeping husband.
