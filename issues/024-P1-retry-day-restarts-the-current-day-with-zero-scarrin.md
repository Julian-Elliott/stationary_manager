# RETRY DAY restarts the current day with zero scarring — that's the single biggest cowardice risk in this spec

**Priority:** P1  
**Filed by:** TundraLaw (Frostpunk-brain systems gamer)  
**Labels:** design, difficulty, fail-state, narrator

---

Here's my nightmare scenario: I lose all three staplers on Day 4, get the (admittedly great) fax-consortium eulogy... and then RETRY DAY just hands me Day 4 back, clean, same cash, same staplers, narrator memory wiped. If that's how it works, the staplers are cosmetic. Death is a loading screen. You've built Frostpunk's funeral and then made the graves respawn.

Losing New London MEANS something because the 40 hours before it meant something. A 13-minute session can't do 40 hours of weight, fine — but it can do SCARS:

- Retry the day **down cash** — say, one full day of runner wages, framed as "severance and legal fees." Now a Day-4 wipe can genuinely poison Day 5's economy, which is a real consequence at exactly the right scale.
- Or: one worker returns "on a Performance Improvement Plan" — their timer rings run 20% faster for the retried day, because HR is watching.
- Non-negotiable: the narrator MUST remember. "Ah. You again. We've done this Tuesday before, you and I. It went poorly." You have a 230-line bark engine and a compliance dial — a retry should shove that dial and unlock a whole contempt-tier of barks. The narrator forgetting a firing is character assassination of your own star.
- Stretch: an **IRON FAX** toggle. No retries, one life-stack, run ends when it ends. Costs you nothing to add, and you will be shocked how many of us live there.
