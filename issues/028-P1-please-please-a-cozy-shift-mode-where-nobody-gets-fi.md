# Please, please: a "Cozy Shift" mode where nobody gets fired

**Priority:** P1  
**Filed by:** marigoldPress (cozy games, plays at night)  
**Labels:** feature-request, game-mode, accessibility, cozy

---

Hi! I've been playing the pilot every night this week (in bed, one thumb, blanket up to my chin — I assume this is your target demographic). I am OBSESSED with this world. The cloud is a warehouse. Email is paper. I named a dust mote drifting past Deborah's desk Gerald and I check on him every session.

But: the shrinking timer rings make my chest do a thing. And when a golden stapler got smashed — a *formal strike* — I put my phone down and stared at the ceiling. I know the tension is the point for some players. It is not the point for me. The point, for me, is cardboard confetti and the little ding arpeggio.

**Request: a "Cozy Shift" toggle** (in Settings, or better: the narrator offers it after your first firing, extremely condescendingly):

- Requests never expire; the timer ring becomes a gentle "whenever you get a chance" clock
- Golden staplers stay on the shelf, purely decorative
- The narrator is free to be disgusted about the lack of stakes. Honestly, him sneering "nothing here matters and yet you persist" IS my kind of stakes
- Keep the report card. I want my B-minus. I have *earned* my B-minus

I would play this mode forever. Which I realize is the opposite of a 13-minute pilot, but consider that a compliment.
