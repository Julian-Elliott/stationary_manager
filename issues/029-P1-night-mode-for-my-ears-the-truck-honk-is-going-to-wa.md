# Night mode for my ears: the truck honk is going to wake my husband

**Priority:** P1  
**Filed by:** marigoldPress (cozy games, plays at night)  
**Labels:** audio, mobile, accessibility, settings

---

Where I play: in bed, 11:40pm, brightness at 20%, one thumb, spouse asleep fourteen inches away. I suspect this describes a lot of us.

Where the truck honk plays: at full triumphant volume, 8–15 seconds after I've forgotten I ordered anything — i.e., under maximum jump-scare conditions. The expiring-request klaxons are worse. They are engineered to spike cortisol, and they work beautifully, please stop.

**Requests:**

- A master volume slider plus a separate "alerts" slider, remembered between sessions
- A **"Library Voice"** toggle: the truck doesn't honk, it pulls up and the driver *clears his throat*. The klaxon becomes a polite paper note that slides on-screen reading "urgent-ish." The information survives; the adrenaline doesn't
- A haptics-only option — a soft double-buzz for deliveries would honestly be nicer than the honk anyway
- Whatever you do, protect the ding arpeggio and the coin fountain. Those may remain at any volume. Those are medicinal

We nighttime players are quiet but we are MANY, and we cannot currently play your game without headphones and a guilty conscience.
