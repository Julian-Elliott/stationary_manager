# Make the NARRATIVE COMPLIANCE dial a real resource — directives should sometimes be traps

**Priority:** P2  
**Filed by:** TundraLaw (Frostpunk-brain systems gamer)  
**Labels:** narrator, design, enhancement, balance

---

The brass dial from -5 to +5 might be my favorite line in the whole spec. But as written I can't tell if it's a mechanic or a mood ring, so here's the systems-gamer ask: make dial position something I SPEND, and make obedience occasionally WRONG.

- **Directives as traps:** when the narrator purrs "Order the stamps. Now, ideally," sometimes I should NOT need stamps. Obeying burns budget I need for the paper storm; defying burns dial. Now every directive is a read — is he helping, testing, or bored? — and compliance vs. cash becomes a genuine two-currency tradeoff instead of a flavor meter. This pairs beautifully with a tight economy (see my other ticket): a rich player obeys for free, and that's a dead mechanic.
- **Pay off the extremes mechanically.** At +5, he's insufferably smug but "accidentally" leaks tomorrow's memo — real intel on the next event. At -5, he actively lies (the day-end report card docks cash it shouldn't, the forecast is wrong on purpose) but his barks curdle into grudging respect, which — given three scheduled Button betrayals and a finale where he cracks and presses it himself — is clearly the arc he secretly wants. Both poles should be viable runs. Neutral zero should feel like the boring choice.
- **The Button's never-press path needs equal love.** Pressing within 4 seconds earning an MBTI stamp is perfect. But holding out all six days while he escalates through three legalistic warnings is the HARDER run, and the payoff — him breaking and pressing it himself — should be the single best-written beat in the game. Defiance-as-endurance deserves a trophy, not just an absence of chaos.

You built a character star. Give him a wallet I have to negotiate with.
