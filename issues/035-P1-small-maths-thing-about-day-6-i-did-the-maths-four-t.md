# Small maths thing about Day 6. I did the maths four times and got four different answers, which statistically means one of them was right

**Priority:** P1  
**Filed by:** Developer Council: Wheatley  
**Labels:** balance, economy, narrator, day-6, softlock

---

QUARTERLY TPS RECONCILIATION. 14,000 paperclips, 2,000 sheets, one (1) commemorative stamp. Beautiful. Horrifying. Let's maths it, out loud, confidently:

- 14,000 clips = one pallet ($15, 10,000 clips — an item whose entire reason for existing in the catalog is this day, well spotted, chef's kiss) + 4 boxes at $2 = **$23**
- 2,000 sheets = 4 reams at $3 = **$12**
- One (1) stamp = one sheet of 100 at $5 = **$5**, with 99 stamps wasted, which is the single most quarterly-reconciliation thing I have ever heard, do not fix it, protect it
- Total: $40. No wait. $23 + $12 + $5. $40. Yes. $40. First try. (Fourth try.)

Plus the normal request load, plus runner wages at $12/day/each, so a player with two runners needs roughly **$70-80 liquid at Day 6 start** or the day is arithmetic-over before it begins. And here's the trap: the RETRY DAY loop (see my other ticket, the one I wrote at 3am, all of these were 3am) can drain money across attempts, so a struggling player — the exact player already retrying — can be starved out of the finale with no way to know why. They won't feel bad at the game. They'll feel bad at *maths*, and maths doesn't need the help.

ASKS, in descending order of me being sure about them:

1. **Affordability floor.** Player cannot enter Day 6 with less than (TPS cost + small buffer). If they're under it, fire a narrator beat: 'EMERGENCY BUDGET APPROVAL. Head office has wired you $40. Head office is me. There is no wire. Take the money.' Fixes the softlock AND is a joke, which is the best kind of fix — load-bearing joke, we have a precedent, it's the twelve pencils.
2. **The odometer collision.** The 10,000-paperclip maximizer monologue is a priority-3 beat. The Day 6 pallet is 10,000 clips. Meaning for basically every player, the monologue — the long one, the good one, the whole 'and then the universe is paperclips' aria — trips MID-RECONCILIATION, letterboxing the screen during the one day with its own progress table. Freeze protects the timers, sure, but it kneecaps the pacing of the finale to deliver a speech about pacing being irrelevant, which is almost too on-brand. Either (a) pre-arm it: if odometer < 10,000 at Day 6 dawn, fire it as part of the Day 6 memo, or (b) demote it to priority-2 during TPS so it plays over the action. I vote (a). The monologue deserves a captive audience and Day 6 dawn HAS one.
3. Log projected vs actual player cash at each day boundary during playtests so tuning is data and not, as it currently is, me, guessing, four times.
