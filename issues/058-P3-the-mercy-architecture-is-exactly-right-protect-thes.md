# The mercy architecture is exactly right — protect these four decisions from well-meaning 'balance' passes

**Priority:** P3  
**Filed by:** UX/Feel Architect (mobile-first, serious)  
**Labels:** keep, difficulty, session-shape, narrator

---

Praise ticket. Four decisions in this spec are the kind that usually get eroded by later tuning, and they should be marked load-bearing now:

1. **RETRY DAY restarts the current day only.** For a 13–15 minute session, losing a day instead of a run is the correct failure cost — it keeps the eulogy funny instead of infuriating. Do not let anyone talk you into full-run permadeath for 'stakes'. Guard: scripted disasters (THE CLOUD IS LEAKING, auditor impound) should not fire in the first ~20s of a retry attempt; a player re-entering after a strike needs one clean breath.
2. **Timers freeze during Narrator Time.** The game never bills the player for listening to its own star. This is the contract that makes ~230 lines of barks an asset instead of a tax. (See the deferral-window ticket for the other half of this contract.)
3. **The eye-poke secret pays a real resource (one free stapler).** Rewarding curiosity with mechanical mercy — not just a bark — teaches players that poking this game is worthwhile, which is precisely the player behavior the 16 MBTI eggs need to exist. Keep the reward tangible.
4. **Day 1 = 12 pencils.** The joke is load-bearing *and* it is a textbook ramp: one item type, one supplier tier, trivial quantities, zero time pressure — the complete loop rehearsed under a laugh. Intern Gary arriving Day 3 as an automation valve exactly when box volume ramps is the same instinct. When escalation tuning happens, tune Days 2–6; Day 1's emptiness is the point.

One small ask while I'm here: the personalized firing eulogy cites real stats — make sure it cites the player's *best* stat too, not only the failures. Roast, not contempt; the difference is one line, and it's the line that makes people retry.
