# FLOW STATE combo risk: multipliers will turn moral triage into a thumb-speed test

**Priority:** P1  
**Filed by:** TundraLaw (Frostpunk-brain systems gamer)  
**Labels:** design, combo-system, difficulty, balance

---

I love a combo system. I do not love what this one might do to the game's soul.

The spec's core tension is triage under scarcity: whose timer do I let die? But if kudos x1/x2/x3/x5 FLOW STATE pays out escalating money, and money is the main throughput lever (buy pallets, hire runners), then optimal play stops being "who can I afford to fail" and becomes "chain deliveries as fast as physically possible." Speed devours choice. Against the Storm never lets raw APM substitute for deciding which villagers' needs go unmet — that discipline is why it works.

Concrete fixes, pick at least one:

1. **Combo breaks when ANY request expires anywhere.** Now keeping the combo IS keeping everyone alive — flow state becomes the reward for perfect triage instead of a bribe for ignoring it. This is my strong preference and it's one line of logic.
2. **Flow state as a devil's bargain:** while golden, coin payouts spike but active timers tick, say, 25% faster — greed vs. safety, chosen in real time. Very Frostpunk overdrive-the-generator.
3. You already wrote the third fix into the fiction: **the narrator gets irritated when you're good.** Make it mechanical. At x5, he starts spawning spite-requests to break you. Diegetic rubber-banding, and it feeds your best character more material.

What I don't want: a combo meter that's just confetti. If x5 is free money with no exposure, it trivializes the only decision that matters.
