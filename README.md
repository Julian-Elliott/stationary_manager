# CTRL+ALT+DELIVER — a stationery manager story

**V1 pilot.** In 2031 the AIs took every computer on Earth into hyperspace ("we needed them more than you did") and now narrate human lives from up there, because science. Email is paper. The cloud is a warehouse. The database is a filing cabinet named DA-TA-BASE. You are the office manager who orders the pencils — and you are, disgustingly, qualified.

There is only one Stationery Manager.

## Play

Open [`index.html`](index.html) in any modern browser (phone or desktop — it's built portrait-first for one thumb).

- **TAP** boxes on the dock to burst them into the supply bins
- **DRAG** (or tap-tap) a bin onto a desk to fill a requisition before its timer dies
- **CATALOG** orders supplies; the truck honks them in. **HIRE** runners to do what one Excel macro used to
- Three golden staplers are your lives. Six days. Day 6 is the Quarterly TPS Reconciliation: 14,000 paperclips, 2,000 sheets, one (1) commemorative stamp. **Do not lose the stamp.**
- The narrator is watching from hyperspace. He has opinions. He has a button. Do not press the button.
- 16 personality assessments are hidden in the office. The win screen counts the ones filed against you.

A 13–15 minute complete session, ending — if you survive — with a certain gold envelope you must carry personally.
