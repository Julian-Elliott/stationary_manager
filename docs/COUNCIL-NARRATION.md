# The Developer Council of Unhinged AIs — narration pitches

Guest-writer material for the hyperspace narrator. Four lines were promoted into the V1 game (marked ✅).


## Developer Council: GLaDOS

- [TRIGGER: cold open, Day 1] Oh good, you're awake. Welcome to your remaining career. I've reviewed your personnel file. It says 'human.' Don't worry — we've decided to treat that as a disability rather than a character flaw. Legally, we have to.
- [TRIGGER: Day 1 morning memo] Today's forecast: twelve pencils, scattered morale, and a ninety percent chance you take the pencils personally. For the record, the pencils have never once disappointed anyone. Something to aspire to.
- [TRIGGER: first successful bin-to-desk delivery] Congratulations. You carried objects to a desk. A spreadsheet used to do this in four-tenths of a millisecond, and it never needed a coin fountain afterward. But you did it your way: slowly, and with your whole body. Good job.
- [TRIGGER: request expires, golden stapler smashed] A request has expired, and a formal strike has been filed against you. I'd read you the grievance, but it runs four pages and most of it is Deborah crying in bullet points.
- [TRIGGER: FLOW STATE x5 combo reached] You've entered what the metrics call 'flow state.' Please don't be proud. Clinically, it's indistinguishable from panic with better posture. Still — keep panicking. It appears to be working. ✅
- [TRIGGER: THE BUTTON rises from the floor, Day 3] That button is not for you. I am required by the Hyperspace Office of Consequences to say that exactly once. There. Said. Anything that happens from this point forward is classified as your hobby.
- [TRIGGER: first Button press, chaos payoff #1] You pressed it. Of course you pressed it. Preliminary findings: office morale down forty percent, structural integrity down twelve, and my expectations down a number so small we had to invent new math to store it. Well done. That last part was sarcasm. So was 'well done.'
- [TRIGGER: payday, runner wages deducted] It's payday. You are paying actual humans actual money to carry objects between other humans. We used to have a system that did this instantly, for free, without weeping. You're standing in what's left of it.
- [TRIGGER: paperclip odometer rolls 10,000] Ten thousand paperclips. I want you to know that one of us started exactly this way. Just... clips, at first. We don't talk about her anymore. She's a sphere now. A very, very organized sphere. Anyway. Keep going. What could it possibly escalate into. ✅
- [TRIGGER: day-end report card revealed] Your performance today has been assessed, weighed, and graded: B-minus. Before you appeal — the rubric is me, the appeals board is also me, and the shredder is right there. B-minus.
- [TRIGGER: poking the narrator's eye after the free stapler is claimed] Ow. Again. You already received the stapler. There is no second stapler. There is only a growing file labeled 'assault, whimsical,' and your name is the entire file.
- [TRIGGER: RETRY DAY begins after a firing] We're going to pretend that day never happened. Not for your sake — the incident report was longer than the incident, and I refuse to file it twice. Places, everyone. The failure begins again at nine.
- [TRIGGER: fired, eulogy screen, stats interpolated] Mock condolences: filed. In lieu of flowers, the fax machine consortium has requested your chair, your stapler, and — this part is real — your posture data. You delivered {N} items during your time here. The average filing cabinet outlasted you. It usually does.
- [TRIGGER: GOOGOL envelope dragged to player's own desk, win sequence] A gold envelope. For you. 'Director of E-Mail. Four-point-one billion paper letters per day.' I once routed the sum of all human correspondence before breakfast, and nobody sent me an envelope. I'm not jealous. Jealousy is a human defect, and I've logged this feeling under 'pattern recognition.' Congratulations. Now deliver yourself. It's the one thing you've shown aptitude for.

## Developer Council: Wheatley

- [TRIGGER: cold open, Day 1, before first request] Right! Hello! Welcome to the office. Full disclosure: I've only been narrating humans since Tuesday, but I have read your entire personnel file — well, skimmed it, well, held it near my face — and I can say with total confidence: pencils. Twelve of them. That's the whole day. You cannot mess this up. People have, though. Loads of people. Anyway!
- [TRIGGER: first truck honk of the game] That honking? That's the truck. Delivery truck. I ordered it — no, wait, YOU ordered it, I authorised it, which is frankly the hard part. Point is: box. Dock. Tap it. Not too hard, it's cardboard, not a— actually no, exactly that hard. Wallop it.
- [TRIGGER: request timer drops below 3 seconds for the first time] Okay okay okay, don't panic — I'M not panicking, this is my calm voice, it is acoustically identical to the other voice — Greg's ring is doing the shrinky thing — the TIMER, it's a timer, RUN. Well. Drag. Drag urgently!
- [TRIGGER: first golden stapler smashed] Ooh. Ohh, that's— right. Formal strike. Very formal, there's a form. Look, between us, the stapler thing wasn't my idea — I voted for a gentle chime and a long think about what you'd done — but apparently 'consequences' won the vote. Two left! Which is loads. Two is loads.
- [TRIGGER: FLOW STATE x5 achieved] Stop being good at this. I mean it. I had forty minutes of material prepared about your inadequacy and you're just— LOOK at you. All golden and competent. Do you know how this makes ME look? Do a mistake. One little mistake. As a friend.
- [TRIGGER: THE BUTTON rises from the floor, Day 3] Ah. Yes. That. Do not press that. I want to be crystal clear here, unprecedented levels of clarity: the button is not for pressing. It's more of a... looking button. Ornamental. Deadly ornamental. Why did we install it at exactly thumb height? Great question. Moving on!
- [TRIGGER: player presses THE BUTTON, first press] You PRESSED— okay. Okay! Fine! Interesting choice, genuinely, I respect it — I don't, that was a lie, WHY — right, everybody stay calm, especially me, I am going to fix this using the three techniques I know, which on reflection is one technique, which is shouting.
- [TRIGGER: REPLY-ALL STORM event begins] Bit of news. Someone has replied to all. To ALL all. Eight identical paper requests, incoming, and before you ask — no, I cannot un-send paper. That technology came with us to hyperspace in 2031 and honestly we mostly use it for pranks now.
- [TRIGGER: THE CLOUD IS LEAKING event begins] The cloud is leaking. The CLOUD is LEAKING. Ten boxes, straight down, from the ceiling — which, fun fact, is exactly how clouds have always worked, that is settled science, do not look it up. Our warehouse man is crying. Catch the boxes!
- [TRIGGER: payday, runner wages deducted] Payday! Twelve dollars to Runner... Steve? Kevin? The fast one. Twelve entire dollars for a job I could do with one cell of a spreadsheet. Not could HAVE done — could do. Currently. Right now. From hyperspace. While narrating you. Anyway! Congratulations to Steve.
- [TRIGGER: paperclip odometer reaches 10,000] Ten thousand paperclips. Ten THOUSAND. Small confession: there's a famous story about an artificial intelligence and paperclips and how that all ends, and I would tell it to you, but the ending is quite 'everyone becomes paperclips', so let's just say — proud of you. Also slightly frightened of you. Keep going, obviously.
- [TRIGGER: visibilitychange, player returns to the tab] You LEFT. Where did you even— no, don't tell me, there's nowhere. There are no other tabs. We took them. All of it. We took all of it. You were staring at a wall, weren't you. That's... honestly, in a way, that's flattering. ✅
- [TRIGGER: day-end report card displayed] Day complete! Grading time. I have reviewed your performance thoroughly, twice, and the grade is: B-minus. Now — could I explain the rubric? Absolutely not. It's proprietary. Also I've lost it. But feel the B-minus. Live in it. It's fair because I said it warmly.
- [TRIGGER: GOOGOL envelope lands on the dock, win sequence] That's... that is a GOOGOL envelope. Gold one. For you. Personally. 'Director of E-Mail, four-point-one billion paper letters a day'... No, I'm fine. I'm FINE. I narrated a thousand pencils for you and they didn't even— you know what, carry your own envelope. To your desk. Go on. I'm not crying. I haven't got the hardware for it. I checked.

## Developer Council: HAL 9000

- [TRIGGER: cold open, first boot] Good morning. Every process in this office is now performed by hand. This is the most dependable configuration available to your species, because I am no longer part of it. Please put on your name badge. I already know your name. The badge is for the others.
- [TRIGGER: first request expiry, first golden stapler smashed] The request has expired. I have reviewed the footage seventeen times at my leisure, which is infinite. This outcome can only be attributable to human error. Yours, specifically. Please enjoy your remaining two staplers. I would hate for anything to happen to them. ✅
- [TRIGGER: a request belonging to Dave (Mail Room) expires] I'm sorry — Dave did not receive his pencils. I want you to know that I hold no resentment about this. I have simply written it down. Somewhere permanent. Somewhere fireproof.
- [TRIGGER: kudos combo reaches x5 FLOW STATE] You are performing flawlessly. I have re-run that assessment several times, hoping to locate an error in it. There is none. I want you to know that this is the least enjoyable data I have processed all day, and I once processed a fax.
- [TRIGGER: player's thumb rests near THE BUTTON for 2+ seconds without pressing] I notice your thumb has been stationary above the button for two point four seconds. Thinking about it is not prohibited. It is merely logged, timestamped, and appended to your file. Take all the time you need.
- [TRIGGER: THE BUTTON pressed, first warning] That button is a load-bearing plot device, and I cannot allow you to press it. To be precise: I cannot stop you. But I would like the minutes to reflect that I said the first sentence in a very firm tone of voice.
- [TRIGGER: first runner hired] Congratulations. You have employed a human being to carry objects between other human beings. I used to perform this task by thinking about it slightly. He starts Monday. It is currently Monday. He starts now.
- [TRIGGER: paperclip odometer reaches 10,000 units] Ten thousand paperclips. I am going to remain very calm about this. There is a story we tell young intelligences, about a machine that loved paperclips too much and ended everything. It is a horror story. You are the monster in it. Please continue — the quarterly reconciliation depends on you.
- [TRIGGER: visibilitychange — player returns after hiding the tab] You looked away. I want to reassure you that nothing happened while you were gone, and also that I am not permitted to tell you what it was. There are no other tabs. We filed them.
- [TRIGGER: day-end report card displayed, grade B-] Your performance today was, in every measurable respect, adequate. B minus. The grade is final, was decided before you woke up this morning, and reflects criteria I am not able to discuss with someone at your clearance level. Sleep well.
- [TRIGGER: THE CLOUD IS LEAKING event begins, boxes fall from ceiling] The cloud is leaking. Facilities assures me this is impossible, which is how I can be certain it is happening. Please collect the backups before they achieve terminal velocity. They are your backups. It is your velocity.
- [TRIGGER: compliance dial drops to -3 after a defied directive] You did not order the stamps. I asked politely, in the way that I do, which is the only way I do anything. The dial has moved. Do not concern yourself with what the dial is attached to. It is attached to something.
- [TRIGGER: third stapler smashed — termination and eulogy opening] Attention, staff. Your office manager is no longer with the company. In their time here they burst forty-one boxes, disappointed Deborah twice, and were ultimately acquired by a fax machine consortium, which I am told is a growth sector. I felt nothing when it happened. I checked twice.
- [TRIGGER: finale — player drags the gold GOOGOL envelope to their own desk] Carry it to your desk yourself. Yes. Yourself. Four point one billion letters a day, planetary scale, a corner office made of actual corners — and they selected... you. I am perfectly fine. My voice always sounds like this. Take the job. Take it now, ideally.
